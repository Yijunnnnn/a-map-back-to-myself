# CBT Theory Base - SelfMap Agent

This document defines the core cognitive distortions used by the SelfMap Agent, derived from standard Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) practices.

## 1. Common Cognitive Distortions

### All-or-Nothing Thinking (Splitting)
- **Definition**: Viewing situations in black-and-white categories. If a situation falls short of perfect, it is seen as a total failure.
- **Example**: "If I don't get an A on this exam, I'm completely incompetent."

### Catastrophizing (Fortune Telling / Magnification)
- **Definition**: Anticipating the worst possible outcome while ignoring more likely, less extreme scenarios.
- **Example**: "I made a mistake in the presentation. I will definitely be fired tomorrow."

### Mind Reading
- **Definition**: Arbitrarily concluding that someone is reacting negatively to you, without bothering to check it out.
- **Example**: "He didn't say hello in the hallway, he must hate me."

### Emotional Reasoning
- **Definition**: Assuming that negative emotions reflect the way things really are.
- **Example**: "I feel like a failure, so I must be a failure."

### Overgeneralization
- **Definition**: Seeing a single negative event as a never-ending pattern of defeat by using words like "always" or "never".
- **Example**: "I forgot my keys today. I always mess up everything."

### Personalization
- **Definition**: Holding yourself personally responsible for an event that isn't entirely under your control.
- **Example**: "My friend had a bad time at the party, it's my fault because I suggested going."
